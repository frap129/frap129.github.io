We Want to Thank Some People

We all had to get our start somewhere, get help from others, have problems debugged, code explained, and surely a lot more than just that. I, Joe Maples, appreciate that, and I'm sure every other developer out there does. I'd like to say thank you to some people, and I'm sure anyone else would say the same. Here's to all the great devs out there.


Team Vanir, and more specifically PrimeDirective and NuclearMistake, for helping me build my first ROM and continuously putting up with my anyoing questions when I was 13.
JustArchi, for help setting up his great optimizations, but more importantly, creating them in the first place.
Matt Bloomfield, for help with silly mistakes and errors.
MrRobinson, for helping me with Aroma Installer and general coding (fucking semi colons)
Team Cyanogen, for great development and code that has become the base of most custom ROM's and continues to innovate the android world
Team Carbon, for great code, features, and help with getting deeper knowledge of the Android source.
Chet Keener and pbeeler, for the out standing SaberMod toolchain that brings new life to ROMs everywhere.
LiquidSmooth, for some great commits and their outstanding build script.
AOSPAL, for their build script and custom host gcc toolchain, as well as some great commits.
ParanoidAndroid, for innovative features and an amazing set of GApps.
CodeAuroraForum, for their Qualcomm MSM Android enablement project that continues to provide better source for custom ROMs.
Linaro, for their super buttery smooth toolchain and various patches.

And last but nowhere near least,
Google Inc. for the development of Android, the most revolutionary mobile OS there is. Without it, none of us devs would be doing what we are today.
